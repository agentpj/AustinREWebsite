    • The home buying process:

    There are a lot of details to be handled when purchasing a home. This overview is designed to summarize the various steps along the path of qualifying, finding and buying a home. It is my job to streamline the steps for you, ensuring everything is completed according to plan.

    Consultation to analyze your specific needs and wishes, your lifestyle interview, your home wish list, the neighborhood of your dreams, your price range.

    Financial pre-qualification or pre-approval You need to get pre-qualified before beginning your home search. Knowing exactly how much you can comfortably spend on a home reduces the potential frustration of looking at homes beyond your means. I can recommend loan officers that I have previously worked with if you are not currently working with someone.

    Select and view properties. It is time for the home tour and I am here for any questions.

    Write an offer to purchase I will review the offer documents and addendums that are needed to go with the offer. The sellers disclosures and other documents provided by the listing agent will need to be reviewed at this time.

    Negotiate the offer It is most likely that some terms of the offer will be negotiated before the offer is accepted by both parties. I will make sure that we respond in a timely manner.

    Deliver the signed offer with checks Once the offer is accepted and signed by all parties, I will deliver the final copy to the listing agent and title company with the earnest money and option checks within a certain number of days stipulated in the contract.

    Schedule the home inspection I highly recommend that you hire a professional home inspector to view the home thoroughly to inspect any hidden defects. This must be done once you are under contract.

    Final mortgage application, Underwriting and Loan Approval The mortgage, loan process, and home appraisl needs to be completed and undergo underwriting. The final loan approval will be released once underwriting has been completed.

    Title Company The title company provides the title insurance. Their underwriting department reviews the title exam and the survey. The title company gets with your lender to do an accounting of all costs to be collected from the buyer before closing.

    Closing The buyer and the seller signs all necessary documents at the title company's office. The buyer brings the funds necessary to close and the lender sends the funds to the title company once all documents are signed by all parties. The keys are released to the buyers once the loan has been funded, or the title company receives all amounts from the buyer and the lender.

    Do you have a hardship?

    Here are some reasons why past clients faced foreclosure. Do you have a similar circumstance? Know that you are not alone!

  • A homeowner is going thru a divorce where the working husband has left the wife and the wife is ill, unemployed and is collecting disability. The wife’s disability payments are not enough to make the mortgage payments and to pay for food and bills.
  • A husband is not able to work any longer due to a disability and is in need of regular treatments at the hospital. The wife is unable to work as she needs to take care of her sickly husband. The husband and wife have gone thru their savings in order to make their mortgage payments.
  • Both spouses were living the American Dream by becoming successful business owners until the economy has forced their clients to cancel their contracts because of the slow economy. No business means no income. They were unable to pay their mortgage payments.
  • A homeowner’s mortgage has now converted to an adjustable rate, hence, inflating her mortgage payments to an amount she now couldn’t afford.
  • A spouse has passed away leaving credit card debt and high mortgage payments. The surviving spouse had become very ill. All the money left to the surviving spouse was used to pay the credit card debt and unable to make the mortgage payments.
