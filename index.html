    About Us

    Pearl Jones, is a Real Estate Broker with 18 years experience in Texas Real Estate. After she was licensed in 2007, she received her first designation in real estate, Certified Default Resolution Specialist (CDRS). Pearl is one of the first agents to receive a short sale designation in southwest Austin. Pearl immediately started looking for clients who were in the risk of losing their home through foreclosure. Pearl has incredible patience as the short sale process is nothing short. The average short sale is 4 months for conventional loans, 2 months for FHA and VA loans. Short sale transactions require alot of paperwork, phone calls, documentation, negotiations, and a good understanding of what a lienholder needs in order to approve a short sale. Her due diligence, patience and empathy has made her successful in helping her clients. She has become a resource for agents all over Austin, inviting her to teach short sales to other agents and brokerages. Pearl also has also earned the following designations and certifications: e-Pro, ABR, MRP, CNE, and SSFR (Short Sale and Foreclosure Resource).

    Do you have a hardship?

    Here are some reasons why past clients faced foreclosure. Do you have a similar circumstance? Know that you are not alone!

  • A homeowner is going thru a divorce where the working husband has left the wife and the wife is ill, unemployed and is collecting disability. The wife’s disability payments are not enough to make the mortgage payments and to pay for food and bills.
  • A husband is not able to work any longer due to a disability and is in need of regular treatments at the hospital. The wife is unable to work as she needs to take care of her sickly husband. The husband and wife have gone thru their savings in order to make their mortgage payments.
  • Both spouses were living the American Dream by becoming successful business owners until the economy has forced their clients to cancel their contracts because of the slow economy. No business means no income. They were unable to pay their mortgage payments.
  • A homeowner’s mortgage has now converted to an adjustable rate, hence, inflating her mortgage payments to an amount she now couldn’t afford.
  • A spouse has passed away leaving credit card debt and high mortgage payments. The surviving spouse had become very ill. All the money left to the surviving spouse was used to pay the credit card debt and unable to make the mortgage payments.
